If you have answered YES to these questions, then you may like become a volunteer on the new South Yorkshire DOT (Digital Outreach Trainer) Scheme.

What is the DOT Scheme?

The aim of the scheme is to improve digital literacy in the South Yorkshire region. One way to do this is to tap into the skills that people like you already have, and encourage them to help their friends, neighbours, colleagues and relatives to become more confident and more skilled using technologies, and finding what they want on the web.

What sort of volunteers are needed?

You don’t have to be a technical expert - you just need to be someone who likes to use technologies and the web, and is willing to help other people.

What’s in it for you?

There are quite a few benefits for you if you become a DOT:

  • You get the satisfaction of helping others become more confident with technology
  • You will join a network of other DOTs to get mutual support and advice
  • You will get access to free courses and tutorials, to improve your skills and to gain qualifications
  • You will get a DOT Certificate which shows you have participated in the scheme

How does it work?

  • You first complete a very short online form, which will register you as a DOT.
  • When you give help to other people, you record what you have done on the DOT website, and this record can be used for one of the qualifications available to you (The DOT Qualification)
  • You will be joined to an online forum, where you can network with other DOTs, and exchange experiences and ideas, ask questions, and answer the questions of others
